What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) calculator is a tool used to calculate a person's body mass index based on their weight and height. BMI is a simple measure used to determine whether a person is at a healthy weight, underweight, or overweight.

BMI Classification

  1. Underweight (BMI < 18.5)

    2. Underweight is a condition where a person's BMI is below 18.5, which can increase the risk of malnutrition, anemia, and osteoporosis. To overcome underweight, it is recommended to increase calorie intake with nutrient-rich foods such as protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats.

  2. Healthy Weight (BMI 18.5 - 24.9)

    3. Healthy weight is the ideal condition with a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9. In this category, the risk of chronic diseases is relatively low. To maintain a healthy weight, it is important to maintain a balanced diet and exercise regularly.

  3. Overweight (BMI 25 - 29.9)

    4. Overweight is a condition where a person's BMI is between 25 and 29.9. Overweight people are at a higher risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes and high blood pressure. Overcoming overweight can be done by reducing calorie intake, eating healthy foods, and exercising regularly.

  4. Obesity (BMI ≥ 30)

    5. Obese is a condition when the BMI is above 30, which increases the serious risk of various diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and some types of cancer. Treating obesity usually involves significant lifestyle changes, including a strict diet, intensive physical activity, and in some cases, medical interventions such as bariatric surgery. Prevention and treatment of obesity requires a sustained approach with support from healthcare professionals to ensure long-term success.
